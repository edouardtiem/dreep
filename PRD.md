# DREEP ‚Äî Product Requirements Document

## One-liner

Dreep lets B2B salespeople send a link to their prospects that shows them how much their problem costs ‚Äî in euros, per week, month, quarter, and year ‚Äî with a live counter.

## Problem

B2B deals die between calls, not during them. Salespeople blame prospects for ghosting, but the real issue is they never quantify the prospect's pain in money. The prospect doesn't feel urgency because no one showed them the number. They can't sell internally because they have no business case. The deal stalls and dies silently.

Training salespeople on discovery and pain quantification (MEDDPICC) doesn't scale. It's expensive, time-consuming, and most salespeople forget it after a week.

## Solution

A product that does the pain quantification FOR the salesperson. The salesperson pastes their website URL. Dreep scrapes it, understands what they sell, generates a diagnostic questionnaire. The salesperson tests it, validates it, gets a link. They send that link to prospects. The prospect answers 5-8 simple questions about THEIR situation and sees a visual, animated breakdown of what their problem costs them per year. With a real-time counter ticking while they read.

The salesperson never learns MEDDPICC. The product encodes it invisibly.

## Target User

- **Primary**: B2B salespeople and sales managers in France (MVP), then globally
- **Company size**: 10-500 employees
- **Current tools**: CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive), email, LinkedIn
- **Pain level**: Losing deals to "no budget" and ghosting

## Core Principles

1. **Zero friction** ‚Äî The salesperson pastes a URL. That's the hardest thing they do.
2. **Value for the prospect** ‚Äî The diagnostic is FOR the prospect. It helps them understand their own problem and sell internally.
3. **No education required** ‚Äî Never mention MEDDPICC. Never explain methodology. The product teaches by usage.
4. **The product IS the distribution** ‚Äî Every diagnostic sent = prospects and their colleagues see Dreep.

## AI Components

### Website Scraping & Understanding
- Input: URL
- Process: Scrape main pages (homepage, product page, about page, pricing if exists)
- Output: Structured understanding of:
  - Product/service description
  - Target persona
  - Key differentiators
  - Industry/vertical
  - Company size target
- Model: Claude Sonnet (fast, cheap, good enough)

### Diagnostic Generation
- Input: Business description (from scraping + salesperson edits)
- Output: 5-8 diagnostic questions with:
  - Question text (clear, simple, no jargon)
  - Input type (slider with min/max)
  - Unit (‚Ç¨, %, hours, people, days, etc.)
  - Default/typical value
  - Benchmark data if available
- Output: Cost calculation formula
  - Must be transparent and explainable
  - Must break down into categories (direct cost, time cost, opportunity cost, risk cost)
  - Must feel credible to a CFO
- Model: Claude Sonnet with structured output

### Question Refinement (from salesperson feedback)
- Input: Salesperson's "üëé" feedback on a question + their suggested reformulation
- Process: Adjust the question while keeping the calculation logic intact
- Model: Claude Haiku (simple task)

## Open Questions

1. **Question generation quality**: How good is AI at generating relevant, credible questions for any B2B business? Need to test with 10+ different businesses.
2. **Calculation credibility**: Will prospects trust the numbers? The transparency of the calculation is critical.
3. **One question per page vs all on one page**: Test both with real users.
4. **Email gate**: Should we ask for prospect's email before showing results? (Probably not for MVP ‚Äî kills completion rate. But valuable for the salesperson.)
5. **Benchmarks**: Can we source reliable industry benchmarks to make calculations more credible? Or rely purely on prospect's self-reported numbers?
